\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Chiyoda sits between pedestrian dynamics, hazard-coupled evacuation, and
information-aware decision models. Social-force and cellular automata models
provide the physical base for movement and congestion
\cite{helbing1995social,burstedde2001simulation}. Toxic-gas evacuation work
already shows that information perception and transmission affect evacuation
outcomes under spreading hazards~\cite{makmul2020cellular,liu2021toxicgas}.
Bayesian and game-theoretic models study incomplete information and route
choice under congestion~\cite{wang2023bayesian}. Social-group ABM surveys
emphasize communication and collective behavior while documenting gaps in
behavioral realism and validation~\cite{templeton2023social,senanayake2024agent}.

The distinction is that Chiyoda treats communication as an experimental control
surface. It does not only ask whether agents possess information; it asks how
an emergency system should allocate limited communication budget under
uncertainty. This makes message timing, targeting, credibility, and spatial
reach comparable interventions with measurable safety tradeoffs.
